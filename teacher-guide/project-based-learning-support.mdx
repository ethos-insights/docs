Trek is built to complement project-based learning by giving students the research tools, writing scaffolds, and feedback loops they need to produce high-quality work.

## What PBL looks like in Trek

A typical PBL cycle in Trek involves:

1. **Driving question** — Introduce the unit's central question. You can pin it at the top of a Trek assignment so students see it every time they open their work.
2. **Research and inquiry** — Students use Trek's integrated reading library and sourcing tools to gather evidence.
3. **Drafting and revision** — Students write, receive automated feedback, and revise iteratively before submitting.
4. **Reflection** — Built-in reflection prompts help students connect their learning to the driving question.

## Setting up a PBL assignment

1. Go to **Assignments → New Assignment**.
2. Choose **Project** as the assignment type.
3. Add a driving question in the **Project framing** field.
4. Attach any source documents, rubrics, or scaffolds under **Resources**.
5. Set milestones (e.g., outline due, first draft due, final submission) using the **Milestone** feature.
6. Assign to one or more class sections and publish.

## Using milestones

Milestones let you break a long project into checkpoints. Students see their progress through each milestone, and you receive notifications when they complete each stage.

- Milestones can have individual due dates separate from the final deadline.
- You can leave targeted comments at each milestone before students continue.

## Rubrics

Trek supports standard rubrics and single-point rubrics. Attach a rubric to any project assignment and Trek will display it to students as they write.

<Tip>
  Share the rubric with students at the start of the project, not just at submission. Students who reference it throughout the process consistently produce stronger final drafts.
</Tip>
