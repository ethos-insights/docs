Trek includes proofreading tools that help students improve their writing before submission. These tools are designed to guide revision rather than correct automatically, so students build real editing skills.

## Built-in proofreading features

### Spell check

Basic spell check highlights misspelled words as students type. Students must select the correct spelling themselves. Spell check is always on and cannot be disabled at the student level.

### Grammar suggestions

Trek's grammar tool highlights potential issues including:

- Subject-verb agreement errors
- Comma splices and run-on sentences
- Incorrect verb tense
- Pronoun-antecedent disagreement

Suggestions appear as underlined text. Students click to see the suggestion and decide whether to accept it.

### Automated writing feedback (AWF)

Trek's AWF tool provides paragraph-level feedback on:

- **Claim clarity** — Is the student's main point clear?
- **Evidence use** — Has the student cited evidence from the source material?
- **Elaboration** — Has the student explained how the evidence supports their claim?
- **Sentence variety** — Does the writing use a mix of sentence structures?

AWF feedback is available after each paragraph or for the full draft, depending on your assignment settings.

## Configuring proofreading tools per assignment

You can control which tools are available to students for a specific assignment:

1. Open the assignment and go to **Settings → Writing tools**.
2. Toggle individual tools on or off:
   - Spell check (on by default)
   - Grammar suggestions (on by default)
   - Automated writing feedback (on by default)
   - Thesaurus (off by default)
3. Save settings.

Turning tools off for a quiz or assessment ensures students work independently.

## Using proofreading as a teaching tool

<Tip>
  Before students submit, ask them to run AWF on their draft and respond to at least one suggestion in writing. This metacognitive step builds editing habits and gives you insight into their revision process.
</Tip>

You can also pull up AWF feedback on a student's work during a writing conference to guide the conversation. Navigate to **Assignments → \[Assignment\] → \[Student\] → Feedback tab**.

## Proofreading and ELL students

For English language learner students, Trek's grammar suggestions can be especially useful. Consider pairing AWF feedback with explicit mini-lessons on the grammar patterns your ELL students encounter most frequently.
