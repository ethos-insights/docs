title: "Tools for Differentiated Instruction"
description: "Trek features that help you meet students where they are."
---

Trek includes several built-in tools to help you differentiate instruction without creating duplicate assignments.

## Reading level adjustment

Trek's text library includes articles at multiple Lexile levels. When creating an assignment, you can:

- Assign the **same article at different levels** to different students — all students see one assignment, but the text adjusts to each student's reading level.
- Set a **default level** for the class and manually override it for individual students.

To set reading levels, go to **Roster → \[Student name\] → Reading level**.

## Scaffolded writing supports

For students who need additional structure:

- **Sentence starters** — Enable pre-written sentence starters for any assignment from **Assignment settings → Writing supports**.
- **Graphic organizers** — Attach a graphic organizer as a pre-writing step before the main writing task.
- **Word banks** — Add a custom word bank of vocabulary terms students can reference while writing.

## Extended time and accommodations

Trek supports individual accommodations at the assignment level:

1. Open the assignment and go to **Student overrides**.
2. Find the student and adjust the due date, time limit (if enabled), or available supports.
3. Changes apply only to that student and are not visible to the rest of the class.

## Small group assignments

You can create an assignment visible only to a subset of students:

1. On the **Assign** step, choose **Specific students** instead of the whole class.
2. Select the students for this group.
3. Publish as normal.

This is useful for intervention groups, enrichment tasks, or re-teaching specific skills.

## Progress monitoring

The **Class overview** dashboard shows each student's current assignment status, reading level performance, and writing quality trends. Use this view to identify students who may need additional support before they fall behind.
