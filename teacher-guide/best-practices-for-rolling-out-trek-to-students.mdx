---
title: "Best Practices for Rolling Out Trek to Students"
description: "A practical guide to introducing Trek in your classroom for the first time."
---

A successful rollout sets the tone for how students engage with Trek all year. These practices are based on what works for teachers in real classrooms.

## Before the first day

- **Verify accounts** — Confirm every student can log in before the first Trek session. Check with your admin if any accounts are missing (see [Student Rostering Requirements](/getting-started/student-rostering-requirements)).
- **Set up your first assignment** — Have a low-stakes introductory assignment ready. The goal is familiarity, not assessment.
- **Review technical requirements** — Make sure student devices and your classroom network meet [Trek's technical requirements](/getting-started/technical-requirements).

## Day 1: Introduction session

1. **Model the login** — Walk through logging in together. Use your projector or screen share so students can follow along.
2. **Tour the interface** — Show students the main navigation: assignments, library, and profile.
3. **Complete the intro assignment together** — Do the first assignment as a class, narrating your thinking. This reduces anxiety and answers common questions before students work independently.
4. **Set expectations** — Tell students what Trek is for, how often they'll use it, and how you'll use their data.

## First two weeks

- Keep assignments short and familiar in topic.
- Check the **Class overview** dashboard daily to catch students who are stuck.
- Provide a visible reference (poster or slide) with basic navigation steps.
- Celebrate early completion and good effort in feedback comments.

## Common pitfalls to avoid

| Pitfall | What to do instead |
| --- | --- |
| Assigning Trek as homework before students know the tool | Use Trek in class first; assign homework only after students are comfortable |
| Giving students a complex assignment on day one | Start with a guided, low-stakes task |
| Skipping the model login step | Always walk through login together on day one |
| Not checking who's stuck | Review the Class overview dashboard in the first week |

## Building a Trek routine

Students thrive when Trek use is predictable. Consider a weekly structure like:

- **Monday** — Introduce the week's reading passage as a class.
- **Wednesday** — Independent writing assignment in Trek.
- **Friday** — Review feedback and revise.

Consistency reduces the time spent on logistics and keeps students focused on learning.
